# 🧬 Comparative Bioinformatics Analysis of Human Lysozyme (LYZ)

## 📌 Overview

This project presents an **in silico bioinformatics analysis** of the human Lysozyme (LYZ) gene and protein using publicly available biological databases and computational tools.

The study includes sequence retrieval, sequence similarity analysis, multiple sequence alignment, phylogenetic analysis, protein characterization, structural prediction, and primer design.

---

## 🦠 Biological Background

Lysozyme (LYZ) is an important **antimicrobial enzyme** of the innate immune system. It hydrolyzes the β(1→4) glycosidic bonds within bacterial peptidoglycan, leading to bacterial cell wall degradation. Because of its biological importance and high evolutionary conservation, the LYZ gene is widely used in comparative genomics and molecular evolution studies.

---

## 🎯 Objectives

The objectives of this project are to:

- 🔍 Retrieve nucleotide and protein sequences of the human LYZ gene
- 🧩 Identify homologous sequences using BLAST
- 🧬 Perform multiple sequence alignment
- 🌳 Construct a phylogenetic tree
- 🏗️ Predict protein secondary and tertiary structures
- ⚗️ Analyze physicochemical properties of the protein
- 🧪 Design PCR primers for experimental applications
