# BLAST Analysis

## Objective

The purpose of this analysis was to identify homologous sequences of the human lysozyme (LYZ) gene and protein using the BLAST tool available through the NCBI database.

---

## BLASTN Analysis

The nucleotide sequence of the human LYZ gene was analyzed using NCBI BLASTN.

### Results

- Query Coverage: 100%
- E-value: 0.0
- Percent Identity: 100%

The highest similarity was observed with Homo sapiens LYZ mRNA sequences. High sequence similarity was also identified among closely related primate species, including *Pan paniscus*, *Pongo abelii*, and *Pongo pygmaeus*.

These findings indicate that the LYZ gene is highly conserved throughout primate evolution.

---

## BLASTP Analysis

The amino acid sequence of the human lysozyme protein was analyzed using BLASTP.

### Results

The protein sequence showed extremely high similarity with lysozyme proteins from humans and other primates.

The conservation of the amino acid sequence suggests that the biological function of lysozyme has remained stable during evolution.

---

## Conclusion

Both BLASTN and BLASTP analyses demonstrate that the human LYZ gene and protein are highly conserved. These findings support the essential biological role of lysozyme in innate immunity and antimicrobial defense.
